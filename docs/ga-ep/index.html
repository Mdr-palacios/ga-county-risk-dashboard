Why this plan Why does Georgia need a dedicated ICE-at-the-polls infrastructure?

HB 1105 makes every Georgia county a forced cooperator with ICE and personally penalizes sheriffs who refuse. That removes local sanctuary policy as a buffer between voters and federal agents on Election Day. Voter intimidation by uniformed or armed individuals near polls is already illegal under O.C.G.A. § 21-2-566 and § 21-2-413(i); what is missing is the volunteer infrastructure to detect, document, escalate, and contest those incidents in real time.

This plan separates roles, defines SLAs, and pre-stages legal and press response so that the people who show up on Election Day are doing one well-trained job, not five badly. It also assumes voters are observing things they cannot legally photograph from inside the 150-foot buffer, so it leans on the Airtable form and the hotline rather than on phones-up at the polling place.

Cross-cutting principles

Five non-negotiables that apply to every role in the plan. Signed annually by every volunteer.

01

Nonpartisan, always.

No campaigning, no candidate gear, no partisan commentary on the line, in Slack, or in any incident note. Partisan behavior gets you removed.

02

Witness safety first, agent confrontation never.

No volunteer physically intervenes with ICE or armed individuals. Document, escalate, call 911 only if life-safety is at risk.

03

Only first-person reports escalate.

Second-hand reports and social-media reports go in the tracker for pattern review but do not trigger dispatch. This is the EP standard.

04

Privacy and code of conduct.

Signed annually. Do not share caller PII outside the case team. Do not post incident details to personal social media.

05

Document, then act.

Every state change on an incident gets logged in Airtable with a timestamp and your initials. If it is not in the tracker, it did not happen.

Five volunteer roles

Each role exists because of a specific failure mode the plan is built to prevent. Roles do not overlap; promotions happen between elections, not during them.

Escalation rubric: RED / YELLOW / GREEN

Severity is set by what the witness describes, not by how upset they sound. Severity governs SLA, who gets paged, and whether press protocol activates.

90-minute role-specific training curriculum

Every volunteer completes a 90-minute role-specific session before being scheduled. Curriculum below is the canonical agenda; instructors mark off each module on the training tracker.

Airtable intake: ICE / federal agent at the polls

Five-page form, bilingual EN/ES, one screen per question, with a visual ID card on Page 3 so callers can recognize agency without knowing names. Page 1 is a safety check; the rest only runs if the caller is safe.

Form pages, in caller order

    Behind-the-scenes fields are auto-populated: Severity from Q15 (someone detained) or Q14 ("asking for ID/papers", "blocking entrance"); Region from Q3 (county); Deduplication key from concatenate(County, Polling place, Date, hour); Pattern flag set when 3+ reports cluster at one precinct in 24 hours.

    Seven-region response map

    County selected on the form auto-tags the incident with a region and notifies that region's Slack channel plus the regional lead by SMS. Adjacent-region copy is automatic for any incident within 5 miles of a regional border.

    Press protocol gate

    A pattern is press-ready only when all four conditions are met. Designed to prevent broadcasting unverified rumor and burning coalition credibility for the next election.

    01

    Volume threshold

    3+ first-person reports from one county, OR 5+ across the state, in 24 hours.

    02

    RED confirmed

    At least one of those reports is confirmed RED by the attorney on call, in writing in the case thread.

    03

    Detention verified

    Detention or voter-turned-away has been verified, not just alleged.

    04

    Affected families notified first

    Affected families and any detained individual's counsel are notified before any public statement, per NILC/TIRRC standard.

    Onboarding checklist (per role, annual)

    Every volunteer signs this annually. Without it they are not scheduled for a shift.
